A1- Interactive Experience Research Project



Q1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?


The first thing that I paid attention to when interacting with this web experience was the minimalist retro-inspired design. The fly as the cursor was also really noticeable on the light background.

Q2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.


My discrete actions when interacting with this website were;

- Read the “freelance developer” subtitle at the bottom
- Press the power button on his chest
- Scroll to see the navigation menus
- Click the sidebars to see the full navigation menu
- Click on the “About” link
- Read about their background
- Return to the home page
- Click on “work” link
- Browse their works
- Return to the home page
- Click on “links” link
- Return to the home page

Q3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the Projects page as I wanted to see in detail his other projects. I also spent a bit of time scrolling as I enjoyed the animations.

Q4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action in my two-minute interaction was scrolling through the content and clicking on navigation links

Q5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended goal of this experience was to showcase Alanns professional identity and work as a software engineer and developer.

Q6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The goal is communicated through a clear header and links, concise introductions and information, and separate sections dedicated to his projects and information.

Q7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The experience seems to be designed for occasional visits, a couple of minutes a few times a month to check for new updates such as new projects/ progress of old projects.

Q8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interactive experience communicates through his project section and social media that there will be regular updates, encouraging more visits.

Q9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The other media forms that the experience references could be animation, present in the fly animation following your cursor.

Q10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

This small animation encourages the user to be more relaxed and explore the different interactions on the page, inviting users to browse more casually rather than expecting complex interactions.

Q11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

This reference communicates the feeling of simplicity and authenticity, making you feel a more personal connection with content as you explore the different animations.

Q12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Although there was not much that was frustrating, I noticed that the text-heavy sections of the site was quite small and could easily be hard to read

Q13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction was the scrolling to see the next menu. It was very satisfying and paired with the little fly it was quite fun.
